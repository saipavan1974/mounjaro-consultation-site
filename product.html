What is Mounjaro?

Mounjaro contains the active ingredient tirzepatide and is prescribed to adults with type 2 diabetes to help manage blood sugar levels. It is given once a week as a subcutaneous injection using a pre-filled pen.

How to Use Mounjaro

  • Administer the injection once weekly on the same day each week.
  • The injection is given under the skin (subcutaneously).
  • It may be taken with or without food.
  • Follow the dosing schedule and instructions provided by your healthcare professional.
  • Store the pens in a refrigerator before first use and follow storage instructions after opening.

Important Safety Information

  • Mounjaro is not suitable for patients with type 1 diabetes or diabetic ketoacidosis.
  • Inform your healthcare provider if you have a history of pancreatitis, thyroid problems, or allergies.
  • Common side effects include:
    • Nausea
    • Vomiting
    • Diarrhea
    • Injection site reactions (such as redness or itching)
  • Contact your healthcare professional immediately if you experience severe side effects or allergic reactions.
  • Keep the medication out of reach of children.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

Can I take Mounjaro with other diabetes medicines?

Your healthcare provider will advise if Mounjaro can be used alongside your current medications.

How do I store Mounjaro pens?

Store unopened pens in the refrigerator (2°C to 8°C). After first use, the pen can be kept at room temperature for up to 21 days. Do not freeze.

What should I do if I miss a dose?

Take the missed dose as soon as you remember if it is within 5 days of the scheduled dose. If more than 5 days have passed, skip the missed dose and take the next dose on your usual day. Do not take two doses at once.

Are there any lifestyle changes I should follow while using Mounjaro?

Continue following the diet and exercise plan advised by your healthcare provider. Mounjaro is part of your overall diabetes management.
