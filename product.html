What is Mounjaro?

Mounjaro contains the active ingredient tirzepatide and is prescribed to adults with type 2 diabetes to help manage blood sugar levels. It is given once a week as a subcutaneous injection using a pre-filled pen.

How to Use Mounjaro

Mounjaro is injected once weekly in your upper arms, tummy/abdomen (3-5 cm away from your belly button), or your upper legs (front of thighs). You should always adjust your injection site by at least 3 cm every time to help avoid irritation. Be sure to thoroughly wash and dry your hands before injecting.

Before You Inject

  • Take the pen out of the refrigerator and let it warm to room temperature for about 30 minutes.
  • Check the expiration date on the pen to ensure it is still valid.
  • Inspect the pen for any visible damage or leaks. The solution should be clear, colourless, and particle-free.
  • Thoroughly wash your hands with soap and water before handling the pen.
  • Choose an injection site on your abdomen, thigh, or upper arm. Avoid areas with scars, moles, or irritation.

Preparing the Pen

  • Pull the cap straight off the pen and set it aside.
  • Take a new needle, remove the protective cap, and screw it onto the pen clockwise until secure.
  • Remove the outer needle cap and set it aside. Then remove and safely dispose of the inner needle cap.
  • Turn the dose selector to 'flow check' (the first line). Point the needle up and press the button until it hits 0. You should see a drop of medication at the tip.

Injecting the Dose

  • Turn the dose selector to number 1 (this indicates 1 full dose is ready).
  • Hold the pen at a 90-degree angle to your skin and push firmly against the site.
  • Press the dose selector until it reaches 0. Keep the pen in place for a few seconds before removing.

After Injection

  • Place the outer needle cap back on and unscrew the needle. Dispose of it in a sharps container.
  • Do not reuse the needle.
  • Avoid rubbing the injection site. If necessary, cover it with a plaster.
  • Store unused pens in the refrigerator. Do not freeze the medication.

Important Safety Information

  • Mounjaro is not suitable for patients with type 1 diabetes or diabetic ketoacidosis.
  • Inform your healthcare provider if you have a history of pancreatitis, thyroid problems, or allergies.
  • Common side effects include:
    • Nausea
    • Vomiting
    • Diarrhea
    • Injection site reactions (such as redness or itching)
  • Contact your healthcare professional immediately if you experience severe side effects or allergic reactions.
  • Keep the medication out of reach of children.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

Your healthcare provider will advise if Mounjaro can be used alongside your current medications.
Store unopened pens in the refrigerator (2°C to 8°C). After first use, the pen can be kept at room temperature for up to 21 days. Do not freeze.
Take the missed dose as soon as you remember if it is within 5 days. If more than 5 days have passed, skip the missed dose and resume as scheduled. Do not take two doses at once.
Continue following the diet and exercise plan advised by your healthcare provider. Mounjaro is part of your overall diabetes management.

Please Note

This product is prescription-only and should only be used under the supervision of a healthcare professional. Always consult your pharmacist or doctor with any questions about your treatment.
